Why Climate Action Matters: Rising Temperatures

  • Extreme Weather Events: Rising temperatures lead to more frequent and severe weather events like heatwaves, hurricanes, and heavy rainfall, causing destruction and long-lasting impacts on communities.
  • Melting Ice Caps and Rising Sea Levels: Increased temperatures result in polar ice melting, contributing to rising sea levels that threaten coastal areas and displace millions.
  • Biodiversity Loss: Rapidly changing temperatures disrupt habitats, threatening many species with extinction and resulting in a loss of biodiversity that affects ecosystem stability.
  • Food and Water Security: Higher temperatures impact agricultural productivity, leading to reduced crop yields, increased food prices, and challenges in ensuring food and water security.
  • Human Health Risks: Rising temperatures are associated with increased heat-related illnesses, respiratory issues, and the spread of vector-borne diseases, particularly affecting vulnerable populations.
